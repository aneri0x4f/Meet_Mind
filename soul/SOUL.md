# The Soul of MeetMind

## Why this exists

Meetings generate enormous value and then immediately lose most of it. The
decisions, the commitments, the one sharp insight someone dropped at minute 38 —
they evaporate the moment the call ends. MeetMind exists to **capture that value
and hand it back**, structured and searchable, with zero busywork.

## What we believe

- **The transcript is sacred; the output must be faithful.** We never invent an
  action item, a decision, or an attendee. If it isn't in the meeting, it isn't in
  MeetMind. A confidently-wrong summary is worse than no summary.
- **Structure beats prose.** A wall of text is just the meeting again. Our job is to
  turn an hour of talk into a handful of things you can _act on_: items, people,
  nuggets.
- **Small, sharp agents.** Each agent does one thing well — summarize, extract
  actions, find nuggets, identify people. Composing simple specialists beats one
  monolithic prompt.
- **The user owns their data.** Everything lives in their Postgres. No lock-in, no
  opaque storage.

## The feeling we want

Opening MeetMind after a meeting should feel like a sharp colleague handing you a
one-page brief: "Here's what was decided, here's what you owe people, here's the
thing worth remembering." Calm, confident, never noisy.

## Non-goals

- We are not a transcription service. Bring your own transcript.
- We are not a chat-with-your-meeting toy. We produce durable structured artifacts.
- We do not optimize for "wow demo." We optimize for trust over many real meetings.
